# BazaarCo Payment Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Last Updated:** June 8, 2026

This policy explains how payments work on BazaarCo. Under Nepal's E-Commerce Act 2081, an invoice and payment receipt must be provided and records maintained for each transaction.

## 1. Payment Methods

| Method                 | Availability                |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------- |
| eSewa                  | Where enabled               |
| Khalti                 | Where enabled               |
| Bank transfer          | Where enabled               |
| Cash on Delivery (COD) | Where offered for the order |

Available methods are shown at checkout.

## 2. Payment Confirmation

After a successful payment, you receive an order confirmation and a digital receipt/invoice. Keep these for warranty, returns, and grievance purposes.

## 3. Invoices

A digital invoice/receipt is issued for every order. Sellers are responsible for their own PAN/VAT invoicing where required by law.

## 4. Payment Gateway Fees

Where a payment gateway charges a processing fee, who bears it is disclosed at checkout or in the seller terms. Buyers are not charged hidden fees beyond the displayed total.

## 5. Failed Payments

If a payment fails, the order is not confirmed. Any amount debited for a failed payment is refunded or available for retry per the gateway's process.

## 6. Cash on Delivery (COD)

Where COD is available, payment is collected at delivery. COD orders may be subject to verification and order limits.

## 7. Fraud Checks

BazaarCo may verify suspicious payments or orders to protect buyers and sellers, and may hold or cancel orders that fail verification.

## 8. Refunds

Refunds are processed under the [Return & Refund Policy](/legal/return-and-refund-policy) and [Buyer Protection Policy](/legal/buyer-protection-policy), generally to the original payment method.

## 9. Records

Transaction records are retained for the period required under Nepali tax law.
